User Guide

SAR Redact helps NHS practices process Subject Access Requests by automatically detecting and redacting third-party personal information from medical records.

Quick Start
1
Sign in with your username and password. If this is a fresh install, the first user to visit the app will be prompted to create an admin account.
2
Create a SAR by clicking New SAR in the top menu. Enter the data subject's details and upload the PDF or TIF files to process.
3
Review detections on the review page. The system highlights third-party names, NHS numbers, addresses, and other sensitive items. Approve or reject each one.
4
Finalise to apply all approved redactions. The system generates blacked-out PDFs and an audit log.
5
Download the redacted documents and the redaction log from the completion page.
Creating a SAR Request

Click New SAR in the navigation bar to open the request form.

Subject Details

Enter the data subject's personal information. This data is used to exclude their own details from redaction — the system will not redact the subject's own name, address, or NHS number.

  • First Name and Last Name are required.
  • NHS Number — enter in the format 000 000 0000.
  • Date of Birth — enter as DD/MM/YYYY.
  • Address, Phone, and Email are optional but improve exclusion accuracy.

If the subject is known by other names (maiden name, preferred name), add them under Alternative Names / Aliases using the + button.

Uploading Documents

Upload one or more PDF or TIF/TIFF files. You can drag and drop files onto the upload area or click to browse. TIF files are automatically converted to PDF during processing.

Click Process SAR to start. The system will extract text from every page and run PII detection. This may take a moment for large files.

The Dashboard

The dashboard shows all SAR requests with their current status, deadline, and workflow state.

Status Cards

Summary cards at the top show the total number of requests, how many are in review, how many are complete, and how many are overdue.

Search & Filter

Use the search bar to find requests by subject name or reference ID. The status dropdown filters by workflow state (New, In Review, Ready for Sign-off, Complete).

Deadline Indicator

Each SAR shows days remaining until the 30-day statutory deadline. The indicator turns amber below 10 days and red below 5. Completed SARs show a dash.

Allocation Admin

Admins can allocate a SAR to a GP reviewer using the dropdown in the Allocated To column. GPs will only see SARs allocated to them.

Reviewing Documents

The review page shows the document on the left with coloured highlight overlays, and a candidate list on the right.

Candidate Cards

Each detected item is shown as a card in the sidebar. Cards display the detected text, its category (e.g. Person Name, NHS Number), the confidence score, and the current status.

Approving and Rejecting

For each candidate:

  • Redact (or press a) — approve for redaction. The text will be blacked out in the final document.
  • Keep (or press x) — reject the detection. The text will remain visible.

Press the same key again to undo and return to the previous status.

Exemption Codes

When redacting, you can assign a legal exemption code to justify the redaction. Press 1 through 9 to apply a code, or 0 to clear it. Available exemptions include third-party data (s.16(4)), serious harm (Sch 3, Para 2), crime prevention, legal privilege, and others under the UK GDPR / DPA 2018.

Risk Flags

Candidates containing safeguarding or sexual health terms are automatically flagged with a risk indicator. These items require extra care during review.

Reason Notes

Each candidate has a reason field where you can record why a decision was made. This is saved automatically and appears in the audit log.

Case Notes

The Notes panel at the bottom of the sidebar lets you add free-text notes about the entire SAR case. Notes are saved automatically as you type.

Statuses Explained

  • Auto-redact — High confidence detection, automatically marked for redaction.
  • Flagged — Medium confidence, needs manual review.
  • Approved — Reviewer confirmed for redaction.
  • Rejected — Reviewer decided to keep the text visible.
  • Excluded (Subject) — Matches the data subject's own details, automatically excluded.
  • Excluded (Staff) — Matches a name on the staff list, automatically excluded.
Bulk Actions

Sidebar Batch Buttons

  • Approve All Flagged — marks every flagged candidate across the entire document for redaction.
  • Reject All Flagged — marks every flagged candidate as kept.
  • Approve Page — approves only the flagged candidates on the current page.

Grouped View

Press g or click Grouped in the sidebar to switch from per-page view to grouped view. This groups all candidates with the same text and category together.

Each group card shows:

  • The detected text and its category
  • How many instances were found and across which pages
  • A summary of current statuses
  • Redact All and Keep All buttons to batch-update every instance at once

Click any instance within a group to jump to that page. Press g again to return to per-page view.

Visual Tools

Category Colour Legend

Each PII category is shown in a distinct colour on the PDF. Press l or click Legend to see the colour key. The highlight fill indicates the category (e.g. purple for names, blue for NHS numbers) while the border style indicates the status (solid for redact, dashed for rejected, dotted for excluded).

Page Navigator

Press m or click Map to open the page navigator. This shows a compact grid of page badges:

  • Green — all candidates reviewed
  • Amber — has flagged items still needing review
  • Grey — no candidates on this page
  • Blue outline — current page

Click any badge to jump directly to that page.

Zoom Controls

Use the + and − buttons in the toolbar to zoom in and out. Click Fit to fit the page to the panel width.

Draw Mode (Manual Redaction)

Press d or click Draw to enter draw mode. Click and drag on the PDF to draw a redaction box over any area. This creates a "manual" category candidate that can be approved or rejected like any other detection. Press d again or Esc to exit draw mode.

Filter Dropdown

Use the filter dropdown in the toolbar (or press f to cycle) to show only specific candidate types: All, Flagged, Auto-Redact, All Redacting, Excluded, or Risk Flagged.

Search & Custom Words

Cross-Page Search

Press / or click the search box to search for text across all pages. Results show every matching candidate with its page number and context. Click a result to jump to that page and highlight the match. You can also bulk-redact all matches directly from the search results.

Adding Custom Redaction Words

In the sidebar, use the Add word or phrase field to add a term that should always be redacted. Type the word or phrase and press Enter or click Add.

The word is added to the site-wide custom redaction list and the document is immediately re-scanned. Any new matches are added as candidates (existing review decisions are preserved). Multi-word phrases are supported.

Detection Settings Admin

Click Settings in the review toolbar to open the detection settings overlay.

Confidence Thresholds

Two sliders control how the system categorises detections:

  • Auto-redact threshold (default 0.80) — items with confidence above this value are automatically marked for redaction.
  • Flag threshold (default 0.50) — items between this value and the auto-redact threshold are flagged for manual review. Items below this threshold are not shown.

The flag threshold must be lower than the auto-redact threshold.

Category Toggles

Use the checkboxes to enable or disable individual PII categories. Disabled categories will not be detected during scanning. Available categories: Person Name, NHS Number, Address, Phone Number, Postcode, Email, Safeguarding, Sexual Health, Custom Word, and Manual.

Save & Re-scan

Click Save & Re-scan to apply your changes. The system will re-run detection on all documents with the new settings. Existing review decisions are preserved — only new matches are added.

Stop-the-Clock Admin

Under ICO guidance, the 30-day statutory clock for responding to a SAR can be paused in certain circumstances (e.g. awaiting identity verification, requesting clarification, or awaiting a fee).

Pausing the Clock

On the dashboard, click the Pause button next to the SAR. You will be prompted to enter a reason (e.g. "Awaiting ID verification"). The deadline indicator changes to PAUSED and the countdown stops.

Resuming the Clock

Click Resume to restart the countdown. The number of paused days is added to the effective deadline, giving you extra time.

Paused days are shown on the dashboard as "+Xd paused" next to the deadline. A full log of pause/resume events with reasons and timestamps is maintained for audit purposes.

Finalising & Download

When you are satisfied that all candidates have been reviewed, click Finalise & Download in the toolbar.

What Happens

The system applies black redaction boxes over every approved item in the original PDFs. It also generates a redaction audit log listing every redaction with its page number, category, exemption code, reason, and confidence score. A diagonal "REDACTED" watermark is added to each page.

Completion Page

After finalisation, the completion page shows summary statistics (redacted, kept, excluded counts) and download buttons for each redacted PDF and the audit log.

Finalisation is an admin-only action. GP reviewers submit their work using the Sign Off button, which moves the SAR to "Ready for Sign-off" status for admin review.

Export & Import (.sarpack)

Exporting

Click Export on the review page or dashboard to download a .sarpack file. This is a portable archive containing all uploaded documents, review progress, candidate decisions, and settings. Use it to hand work over to a colleague or transfer between systems.

Importing Admin

On the dashboard, click Import SAR and select a .sarpack file. If a SAR with the same ID already exists, the system will warn you and show the modification timestamps for both versions. You can choose to overwrite or cancel.

Security

.sarpack files contain patient data. Always transfer them via NHSmail (@nhs.net addresses only). Do not send via personal email, USB drives, or other unsecured channels.

User Management Admin

Navigate to Users in the top menu to manage accounts.

Roles

  • Admin — full access. Can create SARs, review, finalise, manage users, import/export, adjust settings, and pause the clock.
  • GP — review access. Can review allocated SARs, approve/reject candidates, add custom words, draw manual redactions, and sign off completed reviews. Cannot finalise, delete, or manage users.

Adding a User

Fill in the name, username, role, and password (minimum 8 characters). Optionally tick Superuser for elevated admin privileges.

Resetting Passwords

Enter a new password in the reset field next to any user and click Reset password.

Deleting Users

Click the delete button next to any user (you cannot delete your own account). A confirmation prompt will appear before deletion.

Settings Page

Staff List

Navigate to Settings in the top menu. The staff list contains names of all practice staff. These names are automatically excluded from redaction — since they are your own staff, they do not need to be redacted from patient records.

Add staff members by entering their full name and optionally their role (e.g. "Practice Manager"). Click the remove button to delete a staff member from the list.

Custom Redaction Words

Words or phrases added here are always auto-redacted in every document processed by the system. This is useful for organisation-specific terms that should never appear in disclosed records.

Each word can be marked as case-sensitive or any case (default). Click remove to delete a word from the list.

Workflow Statuses

Each SAR progresses through four workflow stages:

New

SAR has been created and documents processed. Detection is complete. Awaiting a reviewer to begin work.

→
In Review

A reviewer is actively working through candidates. The SAR has been allocated to a GP or is being reviewed by an admin.

→
Ready for Sign-off

The GP reviewer has completed their review and clicked Sign Off. An admin now needs to verify the work and finalise.

→
Complete

Redactions have been applied. Redacted PDFs and the audit log are available for download and disclosure to the data subject.
